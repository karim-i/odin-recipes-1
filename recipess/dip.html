David's Spinach Artichoke Dip

Description

With a rather short history starting in the 50's, Spinach and artichoke dip became prominent in due to the widespread use of TV's in every household living room. Companies like Lays pushed this recipe due to it complimenting their products perfectly.

Unlike its processed ancestors, this recipe made by David puts together more natural products for a fresh and creamy homemade spinach and artichoke dip for any occasion!

Ingredients

Now with the history and specialty of todays recipe out of the way, we can finally get into how you can make this awfully American dip!

  • 1 (8 ounce) package cream cheese, softened
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Romano cheese
  • 1 clove garlic, peeled and minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic salt
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 (14 ounce) can artichoke hearts, drained and chopped
  • 1/2 cup frozen chopped spinach, thawed and drained
  • 1/4 cup shredded mozzarella cheese

Steps

Gathered all of your ingredients? No? You just want to read the rest cause you're nosy? Ok I guess we can keep going then! Remember to read all of the steps before you begin cooking.

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Lightly grease a small baking dish.
  2. In a medium bowl, mix together cream cheese, mayonnaise, Parmesan cheese, Romano cheese, garlic, basil, garlic salt, salt and pepper. Gently stir in artichoke hearts and spinach.
  3. Transfer the mixture to the prepared baking dish. Top with mozzarella cheese. Bake in the preheated oven 25 minutes, until bubbly and lightly browned.
