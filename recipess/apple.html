Uncle Karim's Apple Pie

Description

This classic holiday staple has a very long history in the home the Queen herself, England! Though it may seem like a ordinary treat today, it was very much a delicacy reserved for special occasions. Though it originated in England, the English bakers had culinary influences from France, the Netherlands, and even the Ottoman Empire as early as the year 1390.

Now with the history of apple pie out of the way, it is time to elaborate why this recipe is called Uncle Karim's Apple Pie! It all starts with Karim's inability to cook. He makes the same simple dishes every time he cooks. Of which aren't very flavorful or stand out in any way. This leads Karim to take a backseat as his two roommates do all of the cooking for the group. His baking passion started as a way for him to contribute in the kitchen. What makes Uncle Karim's Apple Pie different is the addition of walnuts!

Ingredients

Now with that being said, below are the ingredients that are needed for todays recipe!

  • 9 inch double crust pie
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 3 tbl all-purpose flower
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 1/2 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 cup of Walnut half's and pieces
  • 8 Granny Smith apples - peeled, cored, sliced

Steps

After collecting all of the necessary ingredients, it is finally time to make the pie!

  1. Preheat oven to 425 degrees F (220 degrees C). Melt the butter in a saucepan. Stir in flour to form a paste. Add water, white sugar and brown sugar, and bring to a boil. Reduce temperature and let simmer.
  2. Place the bottom crust in your pan. Fill with apples, mounded slightly. Sprinkle walnuts haphazardly between apple pieces. Cover with a lattice work crust. Gently pour the sugar and butter liquid over the crust. Pour slowly so that it does not run off.
  3. Bake 15 minutes in the preheated oven. Reduce the temperature to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Continue baking for 35 to 45 minutes, until apples are soft.
  4. Take pie out and leave to cool for 20 minutes.
  5. The first slices of Uncle Karim's Walnut Apple Pie must be eaten with loved ones. After that its all yours to be devoured at odd hours of the days ahead.

Conclusion

Wow! I can't believe it but you finished it! You completed Uncle Karim's Walnut Apple Pie! This pie will last in the fridge for up to two weeks, if it can last that long!
